Section VI

Key Concepts to Master

Concept 01
Atomic Number = Identity
The number of protons defines the element absolutely. Change the proton count and you have a different element entirely — no exceptions.
11 protons = sodium (Na), always  •  82 protons = lead (Pb), always
Concept 02
Molar Mass — g/mol
The mass of one mole (6.02 × 10²³ atoms) of an element or compound. Allows you to convert between grams and number of particles.
NaCl = 58.5 g/mol  •  1 mole of table salt per serving would be lethal
Concept 03
Essential vs. Toxic Elements
Both iron (Fe) and lead (Pb) are metals that appear in food. One is required for life; the other causes irreversible harm at any detectable level.
Fe deficiency = anemia  •  Pb exposure = permanent neurological damage
Section VII

Common Questions

Does it matter which food I choose for Lab 2?
No — choose any food from your kitchen or dorm room. The goal is to practice identifying elements from a real nutrition label and comparing them to the Bracket City Food Bank Inventory. An interesting or unexpected result makes for a stronger analysis.
What is the Discussion Board for this week if it is not part of the lab?
It is a peer learning space. Share what you found in your food investigation, ask questions about elements you are unsure about, and read what classmates discovered. It will help you think more critically about your own analysis before you write the full report.
How do I use AI for the lab analysis?
Follow the guidelines posted in the Lab 2 instructions. AI is a tool for organizing and interpreting data — you still need to understand and be able to explain what it produces. Your analysis should reflect your own chemical reasoning, not just an AI-generated paragraph.
When is the full Lab 2 report due?
The data tables are due at the end of Week 3. The full written report is due at the end of Week 4, giving you time to write a quality analysis after completing the investigation.
